01 / DISCOVERY

The Public Ledger Flaw

In traditional blockchains like Bitcoin, every transaction is completely transparent. Observers can see exactly who sent money, to whom, and the exact amount. It functions like a public bulletin board.
02 / SENDER PRIVACY

Ring Signatures

Beldex solves sender exposure by mathematically mixing your transaction with past outputs from the blockchain. To an outside observer, a group of 10 different wallets appears to have sent the funds simultaneously.
03 / AMOUNT PRIVACY

RingCT

Confidential Transactions (RingCT) encrypt the actual BDX amount being sent. Using Pedersen Commitments, the network validates that the accounting math is correct without ever decrypting the numbers.
04 / RECEIVER PRIVACY

Stealth Addresses

You never send funds directly to someone's public Beldex address. Instead, the sender's wallet generates a random, one-time "Stealth Clone" address on the blockchain. Only the receiver has the private key to unlock it and see it belongs to them.
05 / META PRIVACY

BelNet Onion Routing

To prevent ISPs or hackers from tracking your IP address, your transaction data is encrypted in layers and bounced through 3 random Beldex Masternodes across the globe. Each hop peels one encryption layer until it reaches its final destination.
