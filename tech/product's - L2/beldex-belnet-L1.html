Why each layer matters

üéØ
Each node sees only ONE step

No single relay ever knows both who sent it and where it is going. These two pieces of information are always kept apart.

üõ°Ô∏è
Compromising one node reveals nothing

Even if an attacker controlled one relay, they still could not identify you. They would need to control all three at the same time nearly impossible across 2200+ community-run nodes.

üåç
Websites see an exit node, not you

When the website responds, it sends data to the exit node's IP address not yours. You are completely invisible to every destination.

Why it is called "onion routing"

Just like peeling an onion layer by layer BelNet wraps your data in layers of encryption. Each relay peels exactly one layer, sees only their step, and passes the rest forward. Nobody ever sees all the layers at once.
