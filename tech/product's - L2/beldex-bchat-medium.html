Chapter 04

The 3-hop journey:
why your message cannot be traced.

Encryption protects what you say. But who you are saying it to, and from where, can still be tracked if messages go directly from you to your friend. BChat solves this with onion routing: your message travels through a chain of 3 independent Beldex masternodes. Each node sees only one step, never the full picture.

Why 3 nodes is enough

Node 1 knows your identity but not the destination. Node 3 knows the destination but not your identity. Node 2 knows neither. No single node, and no collaboration between nodes, can link sender and recipient.

Chapter 04b

Swarm storage: how your message
waits when you are offline.

BChat has no central server to hold messages. So when your friend is offline, where does the message go? It is stored across a cluster of 8 to 10 Beldex masternodes called a swarm. Each node holds only an encrypted copy it cannot read. When your friend reconnects, they retrieve and decrypt it. Then every copy in the swarm is deleted.

Press a step below to start the simulation.
1. Send Message
2. Swarm Stores It
3. Friend is Offline
4. Friend Reconnects
5. Retrieved and Deleted
Key guarantee

Every masternode in the swarm holds only encrypted ciphertext. They do not have the recipient's private key. A full compromise of every node in the swarm would still reveal nothing readable. Decryption can only happen on the recipient's own device.
