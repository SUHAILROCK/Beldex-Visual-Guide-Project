Section 03

Your message is locked
before it leaves your phone.

End-to-end encryption means your message is scrambled on your device before it travels anywhere. It can only be unscrambled by the person you are sending it to using a private key that only their device holds.

Think of it like a padlock. You lock your message with your friend's public key. Only your friend has the matching private key to open it. Anyone who intercepts the message in the middle sees only unreadable scrambled data.

📡

Their private key stored only on their phone decrypts the message. Nobody else can.

Section 04

Your message travels
through 3 different nodes
so nobody can trace it.

Encryption protects the content of your message. But what about who sent it? Normal apps know your IP address which reveals where in the world you are. BChat hides this too by routing your message through a chain of nodes, like passing a sealed envelope through multiple messengers who each only know the next stop.

This is called onion routing. Each node only sees the step before it and the step after it. No single node ever sees the full picture.

Why 3 nodes?

With 3 separate nodes, no single node knows both who sent the message and who received it. Node 1 knows you sent something but not where it goes. Node 3 knows where it is delivered but not who sent it. The middle node knows neither. This three-hop design is what makes BChat messages untraceable.

Offline Delivery
Messages wait safely for you when you are offline. Nothing is lost if you close the app.
If you are offline when someone sends you a message, the encrypted message is stored across multiple Beldex masternodes (called a swarm) until you come back online. These nodes hold only the encrypted ciphertext they cannot read it. When you reconnect, your app retrieves and decrypts the messages. After delivery, the copies are deleted from the network.
