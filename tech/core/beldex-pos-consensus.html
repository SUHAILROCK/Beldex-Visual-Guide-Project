Understanding Consensus

FIRST, WHAT DOES
CONSENSUS MEAN?

Before understanding Beldex PoS, you need one simple idea. Consensus means agreement between multiple computers about what is true.

01🏦
How a Bank Works
When you send money, the bank checks your balance and approves the transfer. One company is in charge. You have to trust them completely.
02⚖️
How Blockchain Works
There is no single company. A group of computers all check the same transaction and must agree it is valid before it is permanently saved.
03🛡️
Why This Is Safer
To cheat, you would need to take control of most computers at the same time. That is extremely difficult and expensive. The network stays honest.
The Big Switch

BELDEX UPGRADED
IN DECEMBER 2021

The Bucephalus hardfork replaced Proof of Work with Proof of Stake. Here is what changed and why it made the network better.

⛏ PROOF OF WORK BEFORE 2021
🖥️
Computers raced to solve a maths puzzle. The first to solve it wins the right to add a block.
💸
Required expensive specialised hardware. Normal computers could not compete.
⚡
Used enormous electricity. Running 24 hours a day, non-stop.
🐢
Slower block times. Less suitable for growing a fast, scalable network.
🏭
Mining farms could dominate by having more hardware than everyone else.
⬡ PROOF OF STAKE CURRENT
🎲
No puzzle to solve. Nodes are selected based on their stake and a random process.
💻
Any computer can run a masternode. No special hardware or mining rigs needed.
🌱
Very low energy use. A masternode runs on a standard server or home computer.
⚡
Target block time of 30 seconds. Faster and more predictable than PoW.
🌍
Anyone with 10,000 BDX can participate. The community runs most of the nodes.

The Voting Group

MEET THE
QUORUM

For every new block, 12 masternodes are randomly selected. One becomes the Block Producer. The other 11 are Validators. Together they form the Quorum.

12
NODES
PER ROUND
⭐ The Block Producer (1 Node)
The Block Producer collects all pending transactions and bundles them into a candidate block called the block template. It signs the template and sends it to all 11 validators. After that, it waits for them to approve it.
✓ The Validators (11 Nodes)
Each validator checks the block template from the Block Producer. They verify the signature, check the transactions, contribute a random number, and sign the final block. Their combined signatures are what makes the block valid.

Step by Step

11 STEPS.
ONE NEW BLOCK.

Every 30 seconds, the Beldex network runs through this exact process. Here is what happens at each stage.

01
Phase One
PREPARATION
The network gets ready and all 12 nodes confirm they are online.
01
Timing
The Network Waits for the Right Moment
Beldex does not create blocks constantly. It follows a strict clock. The target is one block every 30 seconds. When the time arrives, the process starts. Until then, every node sleeps.
✓Predictable timing means the network is stable. Everyone knows when the next block is coming.
02
Setup
The System Prepares for This Round
The system calculates which 12 masternodes will handle this round and sets timing for every stage. If any previous round failed, the system resets here and tries again with a new set of nodes.
↺This is the recovery point. Any failed stage always returns here to start fresh.
03
Sync
All 12 Nodes Check the Clock
Each selected node checks its own clock against the expected block timestamp. Only when the time matches does the node proceed. This makes sure every participant starts at exactly the same moment.
✓Synchronized start. No node can get a head start or act before the others are ready.
02
Phase Two
HANDSHAKE
Every active node proves it is online and ready. Only confirmed nodes continue.
04
Identity Check
Every Validator Says "I Am Here and Ready"
Each validator sends a small signed message to all other nodes. This handshake says: I am online, I am the correct node for this round, and I am ready to participate. Validators also check the handshakes they receive from others.
⚠Without this step an offline or fake node could pretend to participate. The handshake catches them.
05
Participant List
Each Node Broadcasts the Active Participant List
After collecting handshakes, each validator creates a bitset. A bitset is a compact list of every validator it confirmed is online. Everyone sends their bitset. The group compares lists to agree on who is actually participating.
⚠If fewer than 60% agree on the participant list, the round fails and restarts from Step 2.
✓If 60% or more agree, the group is confirmed and the Block Producer moves forward.
03
Phase Three
BLOCK BUILDING
The Block Producer builds the block. Validators check it thoroughly.
06
Block Producer Task
The Block Producer Builds the Candidate Block
The Block Producer collects all pending transactions and packages them into a block template. It signs the template with its own private key and sends it to all 11 validators. After sending, the Block Producer waits for approval.
📦The block template contains the transaction list, round number, validator list, and Block Producer signature.
07
Validator Task
The 11 Validators Inspect the Block Template
Each validator receives the block template and checks it. They verify the Block Producer signature is genuine and that every transaction inside is legitimate. If satisfied, each validator creates their piece of a random number that will help seal the block.
⚠Why a random number? Without it, someone could predict which node builds the next block and try to manipulate it.
04
Phase Four
RANDOMNESS AND SIGNING
A tamper-proof random number is built. The block is signed by all validators.
08
Commit Phase
Validators Lock Their Numbers First
Each validator sends a hash of their random number to the group. A hash is like a sealed envelope. It proves you have a number without revealing what it is. Once everyone sends their sealed envelopes, nobody can change their number.
⚠Hashes come first so no validator can see others numbers and adjust their own to control the final result.
09
Reveal Phase
Now Validators Open Their Envelopes
Once all locked hashes are collected, each validator reveals their actual number. All numbers are combined into one final random number. This combined number is used to complete and seal the block.
⚠If any validator number is missing, the round fails and resets. All 11 must contribute their piece.
🎲Result: a truly unpredictable final number that nobody could have planned for in advance.
10
Signing
Every Validator Signs the Completed Block
Each validator uses the combined random number to sign the final block. They send their signature to the group and wait for the others. Every signature is verified to make sure nobody altered the block between steps.
✅The block now carries signatures from all participating validators. Anyone can verify the group agreed.
05
Phase Five
BROADCAST
The block is published to the entire network. All transactions are now permanent.
11
Final Step
The Block is Published to the Entire Network
As soon as enough signatures arrive, the block is broadcast across the entire Beldex network. Every node adds it to their copy of the blockchain. The transactions inside are permanently recorded. The Block Producer moves to the back of the reward queue and the process begins again.
🏆One block confirmed. The same 11-step process starts again in roughly 30 seconds. Around the clock, without stopping.

Why It Holds Up

SIX REASONS
IT WORKS

Every design choice in Beldex PoS serves a specific purpose. Here is what each one protects against.

🔒
Stake at Risk Means Honest Behavior
Every masternode put 10,000 BDX on the line. That money is at risk if they cheat. Real financial consequences mean operators stay honest without needing to trust them.
⚡
Near Zero Energy Use
No puzzle to solve. No competition. A masternode uses a fraction of the electricity that Bitcoin mining requires. Anyone can run one on normal hardware.
🎲
Tamper-Proof Randomness
The final random number comes from all 11 validators together. No single node controls it. Nobody can predict or influence who builds the next block.
🌍
Open to Anyone
No special hardware. No mining rigs. Just 10,000 BDX and a standard server. This keeps the network decentralized. No single entity can control it.
🔁
Self-Healing Network
If a validator goes offline during a round, the round fails and restarts automatically. The network detects problems and rebuilds without any human intervention.
⚖️
Equal Earning Opportunity
The reward queue ensures every masternode gets a fair turn. Producing a block sends you to the back of the line. No node can dominate the reward cycle.
