Inner Mechanics
HOW A MASTERNODE
ACTUALLY WORKS
From the moment a transaction lands to the moment BDX rewards arrive in your wallet. Here is every step, no jargon.
🔐
1
Always-On Requirement
Your Node Stays Online 24/7
A masternode is a server you run on the internet that never switches off. It holds 10,000 BDX locked as a bond, which proves you are serious and have skin in the game. The network needs your node alive at all times to function. If it goes offline, it gets removed from the active list automatically.
🎲
2
Random Selection
The Network Picks 12 Nodes at Random
Every 30 seconds, a new block of transactions needs to be confirmed. The Beldex protocol uses a verifiable random selection to pick 12 masternodes from the entire pool. One becomes the Block Producer. The other 11 become Validators. Your node could be picked at any moment.
📦
3
Block Production
One Node Assembles the Block
The Block Producer collects all pending transactions from the memory pool. It bundles them into a candidate block with a header, a timestamp, and a proof that it was its turn. It then broadcasts this candidate block to the 11 Validator nodes in the quorum for review.
✅
4
Quorum Voting
11 Validators Check Every Transaction
Each Validator independently verifies the block. They check that no coins are spent twice, that signatures are valid, and that the block follows all the protocol rules. Each Validator casts a signed vote: approve or reject. At least 7 out of 11 must vote to approve for the block to pass. This is called the 60% threshold rule.
⛓️
5
Block Finalisation
The Block Gets Added to the Chain
Once 7 or more Validators approve, the block is finalised. It is cryptographically chained to the previous block and broadcast to all nodes in the network. Every node in the network updates its copy of the blockchain. The transaction is now confirmed and permanent.
💰
6
Reward Distribution
6.25 BDX Goes to Masternodes
Every confirmed block mints a block reward. Of the 10 BDX total reward, 6.25 BDX is split among the masternodes that participated in that round. The remaining portion goes to the miner or block producer. Over time, these rewards add up. The more nodes on the network, the smaller each share, but the more secure the network becomes.
🔄
7
Continuous Loop
Every 30 Seconds, It Happens Again
Block time is 30 seconds. That means this full cycle (selection, production, voting, finalisation, reward) completes about 2,880 times per day. Your node participates in each round it is selected for. The more nodes on the network, the less often any single node is selected, but participation is always rewarded.

Belnet is a private routing network built entirely on masternodes. Your node acts as a relay hop in a multi-layer privacy tunnel. The user's real IP address is never revealed to the destination site.

Message Infrastructure

From Start to Exit
THE
LIFECYCLE
01
Setup Phase
Get 10,000 BDX into your own wallet
Acquire BDX from an exchange. Transfer it to your personal Beldex wallet. Never register from an exchange account. You must hold the private key.
02
Setup Phase
Provision your server and sync the chain
Install Ubuntu, deploy beldexd, and wait for the full blockchain to sync. This can take several hours on a new server.
03
Active
Register on-chain and lock your stake
Use the Beldex wallet to broadcast a registration transaction. Your node joins the active masternode list and your stake locks on-chain immediately.
04
Earning
Your node serves and earns around the clock
Quorum selection, Belnet routing, BChat message hosting. Rewards accumulate automatically. No action required after this point.
05
Maintenance
Keep it updated and monitored
Apply software updates. Watch the masternode explorer to confirm your node appears healthy. Check uptime regularly to avoid missing quorum rounds.
06
Exit
Deregister when ready and reclaim your stake
Send a deregistration transaction from your wallet. After the exit period, your full 10,000 BDX returns automatically. No approval needed.
