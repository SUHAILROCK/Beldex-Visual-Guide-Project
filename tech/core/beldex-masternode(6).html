Section 01

What Is a MasterNode?

Think of a MasterNode as a power station in a city grid. The city cannot function without power stations. The Beldex network cannot function without MasterNodes. They are always running, always serving, and always being paid for their service.

Full node plus: validates blocks in quorums, routes BelNet traffic, serves BChat messages, earns BDX rewards. Requires 10,000 BDX stake.

A Full Node With Responsibilities

A MasterNode holds the full Beldex blockchain. It verifies every transaction. But it also participates in block quorums, routes encrypted network traffic, and stores messages for offline users.

Every MasterNode operator locks 10,000 BDX as collateral. This stake can be slashed if the node misbehaves. The financial risk ensures honest behavior without needing to trust anyone personally.

Section 02

Why MasterNodes Exist

Three real problems in privacy blockchain networks made MasterNodes necessary. Each problem has a direct architectural solution.

🕵️
Remote Node Surveillance Risk

On privacy coins, wallets must connect to a remote node to scan the blockchain. A malicious node operator can log the IP address of every wallet that connects. This links real-world identities to transactions.

MasterNode Solution

MasterNodes act as remote nodes by requirement. Because each MasterNode has 10,000 BDX at stake, an attacker would need enormous capital to control a meaningful share. The financial barrier makes mass surveillance impractical.

🔒
Centralized Consensus Failure

Without a large network of committed validators, a small group of miners or validators can collude to reverse transactions or censor the blockchain. This is called a 51% attack.

MasterNode Solution

2,200 or more MasterNodes each requiring 10,000 BDX make it economically impossible to acquire majority control. A quorum of 12 nodes validates every block. At least 7 of the 11 validators must agree independently.

📡
No Infrastructure for Privacy Apps

A private browser, encrypted messenger, and decentralized VPN need a distributed server network to run on. Building and paying for this infrastructure separately would centralize it, defeating the privacy purpose.

MasterNode Solution

MasterNodes already exist and are already paid through block rewards. BelNet and BChat route through this same network at no extra cost. The infrastructure is decentralized by design and funded by the protocol itself.

Quorum Formation

Every 30 seconds, a new block must be produced. Before it can be produced, the network forms a quorum of 12 MasterNodes. One node is selected as the block producer. The remaining 11 become validators. Selection is random at the protocol level.

The rule: 7 out of 11 validators must independently attest to the block. This super-majority requirement means that even if a minority of validators is compromised, the block still cannot be falsified.
04
📦Block producer collects pending transactions and assembles a candidate block
📢Candidate block is sent to the 11 validator nodes in the quorum
✅Each validator independently checks: valid transactions, correct amounts, no double-spends
📡If 7 or more validators sign their approval, the block is broadcast to the full network
⛓Block is appended to the Beldex chain. A new quorum forms immediately for the next block
Block Validation

This step is where MasterNodes do their most critical work. They verify that every transaction in the candidate block is legitimate. Ring signatures are checked. Stealth address formats are verified. Double-spends are detected and rejected. No transaction can reach the chain without passing this check.

Block time: One block every 30 seconds. This repeats continuously, 24 hours a day. A new quorum forms for every single block produced.

Earning Rewards

Block rewards of 10 BDX are distributed to MasterNodes based on a strict first-in, first-out queue. When your node receives its reward, it moves to the bottom of the queue and waits for every other node ahead of it to be rewarded before it is next again. This guarantees every node earns equally over time.

Pool nodes split the block reward proportionally. If you contributed 25% of the 10,000 BDX stake, you receive 25% of the reward each time that node is selected from the queue.

Validator Selection

Every block cycle, 12 nodes are chosen from the full network to form a quorum. The selection is random. No node can predict or influence which quorum it will join. Press Shuffle to simulate a new selection round.

Why randomness matters: If validator selection were predictable, an attacker could prepare to control a quorum. Combined with the 10,000 BDX stake per node, acquiring enough nodes to influence any single quorum requires enormous capital, and success would only affect one block out of thousands.

Each block produces 10 BDX. The front node in the queue receives its share. It then moves to the back. This repeats every block.
Why First-In, First-Out?

Every node joins the queue when it registers. After it receives a reward, it returns to the back. This guarantees that no node waits indefinitely and no node receives rewards more frequently than others. The mechanism is deterministic. Every operator can predict when their next reward will arrive.

Unlock and Lock Periods

When an operator decides to stop running their node, they request an unlock. The collateral stays locked for 15 more days. The node continues earning rewards during those 15 days. If a node is deregistered by the network for being offline too long, the lock period extends to 30 days.

Running a MasterNode requires both a financial commitment and a technical setup. Both are manageable for a motivated operator.

Collateral must stay locked for a minimum of 30 days (86,400 blocks) from registration. This prevents hit-and-run validators who register and immediately withdraw.

Stakes do not expire automatically. Your node stays active indefinitely until you choose to unlock. This gives operators long-term stability without re-registration.

Section 08

Advantages of MasterNodes

The MasterNode design produces benefits at every level. For the network. For the operators. For the users of every Beldex application.

🛡️
Sybil Resistance

Creating a fake identity on most blockchain networks costs very little. On Beldex, each identity costs 10,000 BDX. An attacker who wants to spin up 100 fake nodes must acquire 1,000,000 BDX and lock it. This makes mass identity attacks financially ruinous.

10,000 BDX per node. Each fake node costs real capital.
⚖️
Fair Reward Distribution

The first-in, first-out queue means every node receives exactly one reward before any node receives two. There is no lottery, no favoritism, no advantage for larger or newer nodes. The system is mathematically equal.

Every node earns the same reward per cycle, regardless of when it joined.
📡
Decentralized Infrastructure

BelNet, BChat, and Beldex Browser do not rely on servers owned by Beldex International. They run on the MasterNode network. No single company, government, or attacker can shut down the infrastructure by targeting one data center or one entity.

2,600+ independently operated nodes across multiple geographies.
💰
Passive Income for Operators

Operators earn BDX block rewards continuously for as long as their node is active and online. The reward queue ensures that income is predictable and consistent. Operators can calculate their expected earnings based on the total number of active nodes in the network.

10 BDX per block, distributed fairly across all active nodes in the queue.
⚡
Fast Finality

Traditional blockchains can take minutes or hours to confirm a transaction with certainty. The Beldex quorum system confirms a block in 30 seconds. Flash Transactions use the MasterNode quorum to lock transactions instantly, making near-real-time payments possible.

30-second block time. Flash Transactions confirm in seconds using quorum locking.
🔒
Incentive Alignment

MasterNode operators have a direct financial reason to keep the network honest and online. Acting maliciously risks losing their 10,000 BDX stake. Going offline risks losing block credits and deregistration. The economic incentives push every operator toward good behavior.

Honest behavior is more profitable than dishonest behavior at every step.
