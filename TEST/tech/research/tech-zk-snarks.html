What Are zk-SNARKs?

zk-SNARKs (Zero-Knowledge Succinct Non-Interactive Arguments of Knowledge) allow one party to prove to another that a statement is true, without revealing any information beyond the truth of the statement itself.

While Beldex already uses ring signatures and RingCT for Layer 1 privacy, zk-SNARKs are being researched for integration with the EVM layer ‚Äî enabling privacy-preserving smart contracts.

Future Vision: zk-SNARKs will potentially enable Beldex smart contracts to execute with full computational privacy ‚Äî users can interact with DeFi without exposing positions, balances, or strategies.

How zk-SNARKs Work

The core properties that make zk-SNARKs revolutionary:

Zero-Knowledge

The verifier learns absolutely nothing beyond "this statement is true." No data about inputs or computation leaks.

Succinct

Proofs are extremely compact (a few hundred bytes) regardless of computation complexity. Verification is nearly instant.

Non-Interactive

Arithmetic Circuits

Computations are converted into arithmetic circuits ‚Äî any program can be "compiled" into a provable circuit.

Why Beldex is Researching zk-SNARKs

  • Private smart contracts: Ring signatures protect L1 transactions, but EVM interactions are transparent. zk-SNARKs extend privacy to the smart contract layer.
  • Scalability benefits: zk-SNARKs compress complex computations into tiny proofs, potentially enabling rollup-style architectures.
  • Complementary to existing tech: zk-SNARKs add a new privacy layer specifically for EVM interactions and programmable privacy.
  • Long-term vision: Full-stack privacy ‚Äî from L1 (RingCT + ring sigs) to L2 smart contracts (zk-SNARKs).
