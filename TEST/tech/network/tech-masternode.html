What Are Masternodes?

Masternodes are specialized, always-online servers that perform advanced functions beyond basic transaction validation. They require operators to lock significant BDX as collateral.

Unlike regular nodes, masternodes provide critical infrastructure services — relaying BChat messages, routing BelNet VPN traffic, facilitating cross-chain bridges, and participating in governance.

Key Principle: Masternodes create a decentralized services layer on top of the blockchain. Collateral ensures operators are invested in network success.

What Masternodes Do

Beldex masternodes serve multiple functions:

BelNet VPN Relay

Form the onion routing network for BelNet. Traffic encrypted and bounced through multiple masternodes.

BChat Message Relay

Decentralized messaging infrastructure — store and forward encrypted BChat messages without centralized servers.

Cross-Chain Infrastructure

Support LayerZero and bridge operations by validating cross-chain transactions.

Network Governance

Operators participate in governance proposals and protocol upgrades proportional to their stake.

Why Masternodes Matter

  • Decentralized services: Instead of corporate servers, Beldex distributes VPN and messaging across globally operated masternodes.
  • Skin in the game: Collateral ensures operators are financially invested, discouraging malicious behavior.
  • Ecosystem backbone: BelNet, BChat, Browser, and bridges all depend on the masternode network.
  • Passive income: Running a masternode earns consistent rewards while contributing to network security.
