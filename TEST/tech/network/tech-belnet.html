What is BelNet?

BelNet is a decentralized Virtual Private Network built on top of the Beldex masternode infrastructure. Unlike commercial VPNs that rely on centralized servers, BelNet routes your traffic through multiple independently operated masternodes.

Each connection is encrypted in multiple layers (onion routing), so no single node knows both the origin and destination of your traffic — providing trustless privacy.

Key Principle: BelNet eliminates the need to trust a single VPN provider. Traffic is split across independently operated masternodes, each seeing only one encrypted layer.

How BelNet Works

BelNet combines onion routing with the Beldex masternode network:

Multi-Layer Encryption

Traffic is wrapped in multiple encryption layers — each masternode peels off one layer and forwards to the next.

Random Path Selection

Routes are randomly selected through multiple masternodes, making traffic analysis extremely difficult.

Masternode-Powered

Existing Beldex masternodes serve as relay nodes. Their collateral stake incentivizes reliable, honest operation.

Censorship Resistant

No central authority can shut down BelNet. As long as masternodes operate globally, the VPN remains accessible.

Why BelNet Matters

  • True privacy: No single entity sees your complete traffic pattern — not even the entry or exit nodes.
  • Decentralized infrastructure: Runs on the existing masternode network, benefiting from its global distribution and economic security.
  • Censorship resistance: Governments or ISPs cannot shut down BelNet by targeting a single provider.
  • Privacy ecosystem: Combined with BChat and Beldex Browser, BelNet completes Beldex's full-stack privacy offering.
