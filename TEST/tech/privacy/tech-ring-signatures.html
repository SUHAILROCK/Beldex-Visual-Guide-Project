Privacy Evolution History

The evolution of Ring Signatures: from academic research to blockchain privacy
2001 , Academic Origins
Ring Signatures were first proposed by cryptographers Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Yael Tauman.
2013 , CryptoNote
The CryptoNote protocol applied ring signatures to blockchain transactions, allowing senders to hide within a group of possible signers.
2014 , Monero
Monero adopted and improved this model, making ring signatures a core privacy feature.

What Are Ring Signatures?

A Ring Signature allows a transaction to be signed by one personbut verified as coming from a group.

No one can prove it was you.

Key Principle: When you send BDX, your transaction is signed alongside multiple decoy outputs from the blockchain. Any observer sees a group of possible senders, but cannot determine which one is real.

How It Works

One real input is mixed with multiple decoy inputs to form a ring. A unique key image prevents double-spending without revealing the real spender.

Ring signatures in Beldex operate through several interconnected mechanisms:

üíç

Ring Formation

When spending, your wallet automatically selects multiple decoy outputs from the blockchain to form a "ring", a group of plausible signers that includes your real output.

In Beldex, the minimum ring size is 11 members (1 real output + 10 decoys). All 11 appear equally likely to be the true spender.
üîë

Signing Process

The signature proves that one private key in the ring is valid,but it does not reveal which one.

Every member in the ring appears indistinguishable from the real signer.

Key Images

Each spent output generates a unique cryptographic fingerprint called a key image.

The network tracks key images to: ensure each output is spent only once, prevent double-spending, without revealing which ring member was real.
‚úÖ

Verification

Network confirms the signature is valid and the key image is unique. Transaction approved.

By enforcing a fixed minimum ring size, Beldex ensures every transaction benefits from a strong anonymity set.
