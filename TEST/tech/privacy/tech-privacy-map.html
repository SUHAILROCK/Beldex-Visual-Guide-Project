Ring Signatures Sender Privacy

First proposed in 2001 by Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Yael Tauman. Applied to blockchain in CryptoNote (2013).

Your transaction is mixed with decoys so nobody can tell who really sent it. Like signing a document on behalf of a group.

  • ‚úî Sender identity is hidden among decoys
  • ‚úî Beldex uses minimum ring size of 11
  • ‚úî Key Images prevent double-spending
  • ‚úî Computationally untraceable

Stealth Addresses Receiver Privacy

Concept first proposed by David Chaum in 1984. Implemented in CryptoNote (2013), mandatory in Monero (2014).

Every payment creates a unique one-time address. Even 100 payments to the same person look completely unrelated.

  • ‚úî One-time address per transaction
  • ‚úî Payments cannot be publicly linked
  • ‚úî Wallet balance stays private
  • ‚úî Uses public view key & spend key

Bytecoin

1st CryptoNote Blockchain — had issues

Bytecoin First Implementation (Issues)

The first blockchain to implement CryptoNote technology. However, it had controversial origins including a suspected pre-mine and questionable launch.

These issues led the community to create a clean fork, which became Monero.

  • ‚ùå Suspected large pre-mine
  • ‚ùå Questionable launch history
  • ‚úî Proved CryptoNote technology worked
  • ‚Üí Led directly to Monero's creation

Monero

Privacy-based blockchain

Monero Privacy Blockchain

Improved and popularized CryptoNote technology. Became the most well-known privacy cryptocurrency.

Made critical improvements including mandatory privacy, enhanced ring signatures, and later added RingCT for amount privacy.

  • ‚úî Clean fork of Bytecoin's code
  • ‚úî Mandatory privacy for all transactions
  • ‚úî Introduced RingCT (2017)
  • ‚úî Added Bulletproofs (2018)

Ring Confidential Transactions Hide Amount

Created in January 2016 by combining Ring Signatures with Greg Maxwell's Confidential Transactions (2015). The first time sender, receiver, and amount were all hidden.
